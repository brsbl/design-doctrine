# Make each visible facet one coherent dimension

Visible filters must represent comparable dimensions; mixed capability, market, and business-model terms belong in searchable metadata rather than one facet.

## Why

A facet becomes confusing when its options answer different classification questions.

## Prefer

- Give each visible facet one mutually understandable classification dimension.
- Keep useful cross-cutting terms searchable as metadata.

## Avoid

- Mixing capability, business model, market, and context in one filter.

## Use when

- Users browse or filter a typed collection.

## Do not use when

- A small flat set has no meaningful facets.

## Evidence

- Rejected filters that mixed capability, business model, and market context; requested a coherent search taxonomy.

## Check

- Do all options answer the same question?
- Would any option fit equally in another facet?
- Can cross-cutting terms remain searchable without becoming filters?
