# Make every piece of chrome earn its weight

Every label, border, section, menu width, and vertical gap must earn its visual weight; prefer compact, content-led layouts over explanatory chrome.

## Why

Repeated corrections reject interfaces that become too wide, tall, labeled, heavy, or structurally noisy.

## Prefer

- Let content and the current job establish hierarchy.
- Use the smallest amount of persistent structure that preserves orientation and state.

## Avoid

- Explanatory labels, borders, sections, and gaps that do not change a decision.
- Oversized or uniformly heavy chrome.

## Use when

- Several persistent UI elements compete with the content or primary job.

## Do not use when

- Removing the element would hide state, orientation, recovery, or an otherwise undiscoverable action.

## Evidence

- Asked routinely used menus to be narrower and flagged supporting sections whose presentation was too flat.
- Asked context content to collapse compactly and match the command palette; rejected heavy actions-panel sections.
- Flagged a fullscreen drawer with excessive blank space beneath its content.

## Check

- Can any label, border, section, or gap disappear without reducing understanding?
- Does visual weight match product importance?
- Is content still the dominant surface?
