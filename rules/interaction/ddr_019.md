# Give each gesture one intent

A drag handle drags; do not overload it with click-to-group, copy, or unrelated actions.

## Why

One gesture target with competing meanings is hard to discover, operate, and recover from.

## Prefer

- Assign one primary intent to each gesture target.
- Expose separate actions through separate controls.

## Avoid

- Using a drag handle as an unrelated click target.

## Use when

- A control supports drag or another continuous gesture.

## Do not use when

- A platform convention explicitly combines equivalent gestures without ambiguity.

## Evidence

- Asked to remove click-to-group behavior and restore a simple, working drag handle.

## Check

- Does the target have one predictable gesture meaning?
- Are keyboard and touch alternatives available?
- Can an accidental activation be undone?
