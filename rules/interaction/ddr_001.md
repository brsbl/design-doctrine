# Keep routine utilities compact

Routine inspection and operational utilities default to compact icon-only controls with concise tooltips and accessible names; reserve labeled buttons for primary workflow transitions.

## Why

This reduces label noise in dense operational surfaces while preserving discoverability and a clear primary-action hierarchy.

## Prefer

- Use a semantically exact icon, accessible name, and concise tooltip.
- Use a labeled button for the one action that advances the primary workflow.

## Avoid

- Large labeled controls for refresh, retry, view output, open source, or similar inspection utilities.
- Icon-only treatment for unfamiliar, destructive, or primary actions.

## Use when

- The action is routine, local, reversible, and repeatedly used in a dense surface.

## Do not use when

- The action is unfamiliar, destructive, or the primary transition.
- The icon cannot express the action precisely.

## Exceptions

- **The action is primary, destructive, unfamiliar, or difficult to represent precisely.** Use a concise labeled control and the appropriate confirmation or recovery treatment. Clarity and safety outrank density.

## Evidence

- Asked to mine thread history into a personal doctrine, illustrating compact icon-only utility controls with tooltips.
- Asked automation-row controls to stay hidden until row hover, while the drag affordance remains discoverable beforehand.
- Requested a compact comment popover with inline counts, no carets, and navigation back to the anchored gutter.

## Check

- Are routine utilities compact without competing with the primary action?
- Does every icon-only action have an accessible name and concise tooltip?
- Would a first-time user understand any action that remains unlabeled?
