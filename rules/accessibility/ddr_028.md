# Make the locus of hover controls discoverable

When a control appears on hover, show a quiet at-rest cue for its location and support keyboard focus plus a tooltip; users must not memorize invisible hit areas.

## Why

Progressive disclosure should reduce clutter without making the affordance impossible to locate.

## Prefer

- Leave a quiet positional cue at rest.
- Reveal the control on hover and focus with an accessible name and concise tooltip.

## Avoid

- Completely invisible hit areas.
- Hover-only access with no keyboard path.

## Use when

- A control is hidden until hover to reduce density.

## Do not use when

- The control is always visible or the entire containing object is the target.

## Evidence

- Requested a persistent drag-handle cue and accurate tooltip so its location is not guesswork before hover.
- Required comment-list interaction to open the comment at its actual gutter anchor, not from a detached list control.

## Check

- Can a user locate the affordance before hovering precisely?
- Does keyboard focus reveal and operate it?
- Is the tooltip supplemental rather than essential?
