# Let content grow within explicit bounds

Let inputs and surfaces grow with useful content to an explicit maximum; do not alter unrelated minimum constraints.

## Why

Useful content should not be artificially cramped, while unbounded growth and collateral resizing destabilize the surrounding layout.

## Prefer

- Grow with useful content until a clear maximum.
- Preserve unrelated minimum sizes and narrow-viewport constraints.

## Avoid

- Fixed cramped surfaces for variable content.
- Changing a neighboring minimum merely to fit one state.

## Use when

- Content length or user editing meaningfully changes the useful footprint.

## Do not use when

- Growth would exceed a viewport or destabilize the primary workspace.

## Evidence

- Flagged a fullscreen surface that exceeded content needs and left excessive empty space.
- Rejected an unrequested minimum-width change while asking comment input width to grow only to a maximum.
- Specified a comment composer that grows as text wraps: compact secondary actions in a plus menu at one line, then inline footer actions while text continues paragraph-like.

## Check

- Is there a useful growth range and explicit maximum?
- Are unrelated constraints preserved?
- Does the narrowest supported viewport remain usable?
