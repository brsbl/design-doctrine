# Use restrained semantic color variety

Use product-native tokenized color with tasteful semantic variety; avoid both childish saturation and blanket desaturation or one-accent monotony.

## Why

Color should communicate meaningful status and structure while remaining professional and native to the product.

## Prefer

- Use the product token system and reserve color differences for meaningful semantics.
- Balance professional restraint with enough variety to distinguish states.

## Avoid

- Childish saturation.
- Making every state the same neutral or accent color.

## Use when

- Color carries state or status meaning in product UI.

## Do not use when

- A deliberately neutral or brandless wireframe is the artifact.

## Evidence

- Rejected saturated status colors as childish and asked for restrained, professional status treatment.

## Check

- Does each color difference carry meaning?
- Does the palette feel product-native and professional?
- Are distinct states still distinguishable?
