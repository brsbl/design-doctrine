# Use dividers to express stable structure

Dividers communicate stable grouping, not transient drag state; avoid duplicate seams and keep related boundaries visually consistent.

## Why

A boundary should explain composition rather than add noise or become a temporary state indicator.

## Prefer

- Use one quiet consistent boundary where stable grouping needs it.
- Use a dedicated drag or selection affordance for transient state.

## Avoid

- Duplicate seams.
- Turning a structural divider into the only drag-state indicator.

## Use when

- A visible line separates persistent regions or groups.

## Do not use when

- The boundary is an interactive resizable splitter with clear affordance.

## Evidence

- Required the row divider to remain stable during dragging and existing fade behavior to be reused.
- Questioned a redundant footer boundary and inconsistent hierarchy between visually sibling action sections.

## Check

- Does the divider explain stable structure?
- Are adjacent seams duplicated?
- Is transient state communicated independently?
