# Make new surfaces feel product-native and real

New product surfaces need credible anatomy, density, and host-product fidelity; do not ship generic system UI or mock-like bespoke compositions.

## Why

Minimal chrome is not permission for abstract, low-fidelity, or visibly foreign product surfaces.

## Prefer

- Use realistic product anatomy and density.
- Ground the surface in host-product components and tokens.

## Avoid

- Generic system chrome.
- Abstract mockups that cannot be recognized as the intended product object.

## Use when

- A design will represent or become a real product surface.

## Do not use when

- A deliberately brandless technical wireframe is the requested artifact, while still retaining recognizable anatomy.

## Evidence

- Rejected generic system-style UI and excessive inputs; asked for a minimal, intentional surface matching the host product.
- Flagged clipped comment hover treatment and a thread interface that looked like an unrelated mockup.
- Judged the implemented interaction patterns insufficiently faithful to product-quality UI.

## Check

- Can the intended product object be recognized without explanation?
- Does the surface feel native beside its closest host-product sibling?
- Is the anatomy credible at realistic content density?
