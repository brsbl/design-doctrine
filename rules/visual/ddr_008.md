# Reveal controls without reflow

Hover, drag, and edit affordances must not shift row or panel geometry; transient controls should overlay or use reserved space.

## Why

Reflow under the pointer makes dense interfaces feel unstable and causes users to lose their target.

## Prefer

- Overlay transient actions or reserve their space.
- Keep the anchor and neighboring content stable.

## Avoid

- Adding hover or edit controls that push content or resize the row.

## Use when

- A transient affordance appears inside an existing row or panel.

## Do not use when

- Intentional content expansion is the action and the anchor remains stable.

## Evidence

- Reported the row's padding shrank when its drag handle appeared.
- Reported entering formula or variable mode shifted the layout downward.

## Check

- Does geometry remain stable when controls appear?
- Can the pointer target stay under the pointer?
- Is intentional expansion visually anchored?
